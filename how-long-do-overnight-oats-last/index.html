How Long Do Overnight Oats Last?

By the OATOLOGY Kitchen · Updated 21 July 2026

5 min read Food-safety guidance Fridge, freezer, and worktop

Overnight oats last 3 to 5 days in a sealed jar in the fridge, kept at 5°C or below. With fresh fruit stirred through, eat them within 3 days. A plain base of oats, milk, chia seeds, and yoghurt keeps up to 5. Left out at room temperature overnight, they go in the bin.

That is the short answer. Below: the day-by-day picture, the ingredients that shorten the window, how to freeze a batch properly, and the signs a jar is past it.

How long do overnight oats last in the fridge?

Sealed and refrigerated, overnight oats keep for 3 to 5 days. Where you land in that range depends on what is in the jar. Our recipes are written with fresh fruit stirred through, so they are at their best inside 3 days. A plain base of oats, milk, chia seeds, and yoghurt keeps up to 5.

Temperature matters as much as time. The Food Standards Agency recommends a fridge temperature between 0 and 5°C. If your fridge has a dial rather than a display, a cheap fridge thermometer settles the question.

Rule of thumb: fruit in the jar, eat within 3 days. Plain base, up to 5. Sealed lid either way, and judge by smell before you eat.

Overnight oats day by day

How many days overnight oats stay good is a sliding scale, not a cliff edge. Here is how a sealed jar changes across the week.

DayWhat to expect
Days 1 and 2At their best. Thick, creamy, and fully set.
Day 3Still good. The texture softens and stirred-through fruit starts to tire. Last day for jars with fresh fruit in them.
Days 4 and 5Plain base only (oats, milk, chia seeds, yoghurt). Stir well before eating and judge by smell first.
Day 6 and beyondNo. The window has closed, bin the jar.

How to tell your overnight oats have gone off

Overnight oats announce themselves when they turn. Look for:

  • A sour or yeasty smell. Fresh oats smell mild and milky. Anything sharp or bread-like means the jar is done.
  • A fizzy taste. That is fermentation. Stop eating.
  • Any mould, on the surface, the sides, or the lid.
  • Watery separation that does not stir back in. A little surface liquid is normal and stirs straight back through. Liquid that stays split means the structure has broken down.

Which ingredients shorten the window

The base is rarely the problem; the extras set the clock. Here is how common additions hold up in the fridge.

IngredientHow it holds up
BananaBrowns fast. Add fresh each morning rather than storing it in the jar.
MangoHolds its colour well. Fine to go in from the start.
BerriesFine from the start. They tint the oats as the days pass, which is cosmetic, not a safety issue.
Greek yoghurt or cream cheeseFine within the 3 to 5 day window.
Nuts, seeds, and biscuitGo soft in the jar. Add fresh for crunch.
Honey, maple syrup, and spicesNo problem. They keep the full window.

Can you leave overnight oats out overnight?

No. As a general food-safety rule, milk-based food should not sit at room temperature for more than about 2 hours. A jar forgotten on the worktop overnight has been warm for 8 hours or more, and no amount of hopeful sniffing changes that. Bin it and make another; it takes 10 minutes.

The soak itself happens in the fridge too: mix the jar, seal it, and refrigerate straight away.

Can you freeze overnight oats?

Yes, overnight oats freeze well for up to about 3 months in an airtight container. Two rules make the difference:

  • Freeze the base only. Leave off fresh fruit toppings and add them after thawing.
  • Leave 2 to 3cm of headroom. The mixture expands as it freezes, and a glass jar filled to the top can crack.

Thaw overnight in the fridge. Expect a slightly softer texture than a fresh jar: stir it well, and loosen with a splash of milk if you like.

How far ahead can you meal prep?

Making 2 or 3 jars on a Sunday evening covers the first half of the week comfortably: the fruit-stirred jars carry you to Wednesday, and a plain base stretches to Thursday or Friday. Add banana, nuts, and anything crunchy fresh each morning rather than storing them in the jar.

Your questions, answered

Can you eat overnight oats after 5 days?

No. Overnight oats are safe for 3 to 5 days in a sealed jar in the fridge, and 5 days is the outer edge for a plain base with no fresh fruit. After that, bin the jar. Even if it smells fine, the window has closed.

Can you freeze overnight oats?

Yes. Overnight oats freeze well for up to about 3 months in an airtight container. Freeze the base without fresh fruit toppings, and leave 2 to 3cm of headroom because the mixture expands as it freezes; a full glass jar can crack. Thaw overnight in the fridge and stir well.

Can overnight oats be left out overnight?

No. Overnight oats are milk-based. As a general food-safety rule, milk-based food should not sit at room temperature for more than about 2 hours. A jar left on the worktop overnight has been warm all night, so bin it. The overnight soak itself happens in the fridge, not on the side.

How do you know overnight oats have gone off?

Overnight oats have gone off if they smell sour or yeasty, taste fizzy, show any mould, or separate into watery liquid that will not stir back in. A little surface liquid that stirs smoothly back through is normal. If you notice any one of those signs, bin the jar.

How far ahead can you meal prep overnight oats?

Meal-prepped overnight oats cover 3 days with fresh fruit stirred through, or up to 5 days as a plain base of oats, milk, chia seeds, and yoghurt. Making 2 or 3 jars on a Sunday sorts the first half of the week; add fruit and toppings each morning.

Do overnight oats need to be airtight?

Overnight oats keep best with an airtight lid. A sealed jar holds them fresher for the full 3 to 5 days, stops them picking up fridge smells, and travels without leaking. A loosely covered bowl works for a night, but the surface dries and the window shortens.
